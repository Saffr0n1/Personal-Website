What I Do

I'm currently part of the Loncar group at Harvard University, where my work focuses on the Silicon-vacancy center in diamond. In this role, I have created simulations of the strain signal within such devices and am currently working to integrate inverse design photonics with SiV-SiN devices. My previous research has included work in materials science at Stony Brook University and immunology at Rockefeller University.

Beyond my research, I enjoy applied machine learning and am working on integrating computer vision with onboard robotics.
